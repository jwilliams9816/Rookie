Pitstops

How to do a proper pitstop

September 12, 2017

One of the biggest things that can make or break your race is a pitstop. If you do not tighten all the lug nuts, get enough fuel in, jump over the wall to quickly or do not do it fast enough you could ruin the race for your driver. But also the driver has to listen to the crew chief and make sure he/she does not speed on pit road.

Materials

  • Crew
  • Jack
  • Air guns
  • 4 tires
  • Fuel can
  • Lug nuts
  • Helmet
  • Firesuit
  • Spotter

    • Steps to do a good pit stop:

    • Crew chief lets crew, driver and spotter know their driver is coming down pit road
    • Driver makes sure he is going speed limit down pit road
    • Spotter counts down how many spots away driver is from pit box
    • Crew waits for driver to be 3 pit stalls away before going over wall
    • Driver stops completly as crew runs to the right side of the car. Fuel guy stays on left side and starts fueling car
    • Jackman jacks up the car, while tire changers take off lug nuts
    • Tire changers take off wheels as tire carrier puts new tires on the car
    • Tire changers bolt on new tires while tire carriers roll old tires over the wall and grab new tires for left side
    • Jackman lets car down and runs to left side with tire changers
    • Fuel guy gives can to crew member on other side of the wall and is handed a second can
    • Repeat steps 6 and 7
    • Tire changers bolt on new tires while tire carriers carry tires over the pit wall
    • Jackman lets car down, fuel guy takes can out and crew chief tells driver to go and spotter lets driver know if there is a car around he could hit
    • Driver makes sure he is going speed limit down pit road as he leaves
    • One or two crew member goes back over wall and picks up lug nuts from old tires
